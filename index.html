Vote Yes on May 2nd for the Warmth and Safety of Benton Harbor Students

What is on the May 2 ballot?

On May 2, voters in the Benton Harbor Area Schools district will be asked to renew a two-mill sinking fund millage for 10 years. The sinking fund millage will generate approximately $1.3 million each year to keep school facilities in good repair. The sinking fund dollars can only be used for specific purchases set forth by the State of Michigan. Funds cannot be used for employee salaries and benefits.



What is a “sinking fund”?

A sinking fund is separately maintained account into which a local school district can deposit voter approved local millage revenue to fund facility projects or repairs and eligible security and technology costs as they arise rather than borrowing funds for these expenses.

A sinking fund provide districts with a cost-effective alternative to borrowing or bonding for some expenses because the sinking fund does not bring interest costs, bond underwriting costs, or hefty legal fees associated with bond issues.

What is the purpose of the sinking fund?

The goal of the building and site “sinking fund” millage is to maintain our facilities over the next 10 years, and to provide safe, comfortable, and educationally appropriate learning environments for our children.

How does a sinking fund help Benton Harbor Area Schools?

A sinking fund helps relieve the General Fund of expensive facility costs such as roof replacements, boiler replacements, parking lot re-construction, and other “capital expenditures” that would otherwise be paid from the General Fund. Relieving this burden from the General Fund would allow more dollars to be directed specifically to the classroom.

Financial Questions

Will this millage increase my property taxes?

No. The requested millage rate (two mills) is exactly the same as the sinking fund millage rate levied in 2016.

Is this a new millage?

No, the District has had a sinking fund for years.

How much revenue will the sinking fund generate?

At current property values, two mills generates approximately $1.38 million per year for the district. Taxable values and tax collections vary from year to year, so the actual revenue collected will vary.

What is a mill?

A mill represents $1 for every $1,000 of taxable value of your property. The taxable value of your property is the same, or lower than, the state equalized value (SEV). The taxable value is approximately 50% (but often less than 50%) of the market value of your property.

How many mills will be levied?

Two mills, exactly the same as the sinking fund millage rate that was levied in 2015.

millage on property taxes?
